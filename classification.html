Heart Disease Prediction

This project used the Support Vector Machine(SVM) machine learning model to predict whether the patient with specific attributes such as Cholesterol will lead to heart disease. This machine learning model will help people detect whether they are at risk of heart disease. With early detection of heart disease, it can reduce suffering and prevents complications such as heart failure.

Photo by jesse orrico on Unsplash

Background


This dataset consists of 918 observations and a total of five heart datasets are combined together to form this dataset. There are 12 columns in the dataset

  • Age - Age of Patient
  • Sex - Male or Female
  • ChestPainType - Type of Chest Pain
  • RestingBP- Resting Blood Pressure
  • Cholesterol - serum cholesterol
  • FastingBS - 1(FastingBS > 120 mg/dl) , 0(less than 120/mg/dl)
  • ExerciseAngina: exercise-induced angina
  • Oldpeak - oldpeak = ST
  • ST_Slope - the slope of the peak exercise ST segment
  • HeartDisease - 1(heart disease), 0 (Normal)

Result

Based on the classification report, The recall value is 0.84. The value seems to be quite good. Recall measures the proportion of actual positives that the model captured by labeling it true positive. The high recall value is critical as there is a high cost of associated false negatives. For instance, the test indicated that the patient does not have heart disease when actually, the patient has heart disease.
