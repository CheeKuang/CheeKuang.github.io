7.Four assumptions of Linear Regression


1.Linearity


In the scatter plot of actual vs. predicted values, points should be approximately aligned along a diagonal line. This indicates that the relationship between the independent variables and the dependent variable is linear.

2.Independence of Errors: Plot residuals vs. fitted values or time


In the plot of residuals against fitted values (or time, if applicable), there should be no discernible pattern or trend. The residuals should be randomly scattered around the horizontal line at y=0, indicating that the errors are independent of each other.

3.Homoscedasticity: Plot residuals vs. fitted values and check for consistent spread


In the plot of residuals against fitted values, the spread of the residuals should remain relatively constant across all levels of the fitted values. There should be no systematic pattern in the spread of residuals as the fitted values change.

4.Normality of Errors:Histogram and Q-Q plot of residuals


In the histogram of residuals, the distribution should resemble a bell-shaped curve, indicating normality. In the Q-Q plot of residuals, the points should approximately fall along the diagonal line, especially in the center. Deviations from the diagonal line may indicate departures from normality.
